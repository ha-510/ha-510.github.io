About

I am a B.S. student in Astrophysics at Rutgers University (Class of 2026), and a Ronald E. McNair Scholar. My work spans ALMA absorption-line studies of active galaxies and numerical simulations of Earth’s geochemical evolution. Long term, I aim to investigate the formation and evolution of high-redshift quasars and their impact on their host galaxies and the intergalactic medium.

Research

ALMA CO Absorption in Active Galaxies

Brigham Young University · Advisor: Dr. Benjamin Boizelle
I use archival ALMA data to search for carbon monoxide absorption against bright radio cores in nearby active galaxies. By modeling the absorption profiles, I constrain CO and H2 column densities and explore the structure and kinematics of cold gas in circumnuclear disks. This project forms the basis of my senior honors thesis.

W-182 Isotopic Anomalies & Mantle Dynamics

Rutgers University · Advisors: Prof. Katherine Bermingham & Prof. Charles-Édouard Boukaré
I modified Fortran geodynamic codes and ran large-scale simulations on Canadian HPC clusters to investigate how late accretion, core–mantle interaction, and mantle mixing can produce the tungsten-182 signatures observed in mantle-derived rocks. This work led to an in-prep manuscript integrating geochemistry and fluid dynamics.
